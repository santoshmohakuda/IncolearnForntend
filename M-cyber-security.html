Cyber Security Course Curriculum

Eligibility

This course is designed for professionals and students with a background in IT, computer science, or related fields. Prior experience in network security or information technology is beneficial but not mandatory.

  • A strong interest in cybersecurity and a willingness to learn.
  • Basic understanding of computer networks and operating systems.
  • Familiarity with programming languages such as Python or Java.
  • Analytical thinking and problem-solving skills.

Pre-requisites

While no formal prerequisites are required, it is recommended that learners have a basic knowledge of IT concepts, such as networking, software development, and operating systems. Familiarity with cybersecurity fundamentals will also be advantageous.

Module : 01

  • - Overview of cybersecurity principles
  • - Understanding the threat landscape
  • - Types of cyber attacks (phishing, malware, etc.)
  • - Risk management and assessment
  • - Basic security terminologies and concepts

Module : 02

  • - Fundamentals of network security
  • - Firewalls and intrusion detection/prevention systems
  • - Virtual Private Networks (VPNs)
  • - Network segmentation and isolation
  • - Securing wireless networks

Module : 03

  • - Introduction to application security
  • - Securing web applications (OWASP Top 10)
  • - Secure coding practices
  • - Encryption and cryptography basics
  • - Authentication and authorization mechanisms

Module : 04

  • - Introduction to incident response
  • - Creating and implementing an incident response plan
  • - Identifying and analyzing security incidents
  • - Containment, eradication, and recovery
  • - Post-incident analysis and reporting

Cyber Security Exam and Certification

The certification exam typically consists of multiple-choice questions, scenario-based questions, and practical assessments. The duration and the number of questions may vary depending on the certification body.

To prepare for the certification exam, it is recommended to follow the course curriculum, complete all hands-on labs, participate in study groups, and review practice exams to familiarize yourself with the exam format.

The passing criteria for the certification exam vary depending on the certification body but generally require a minimum score to demonstrate competency in cybersecurity principles and practices.

After passing the exam, you will receive your certification, which is recognized by industry professionals. You may also receive a digital badge to showcase your achievement on professional platforms like LinkedIn.
