Project Management Course Curriculum

Eligibility

Ideal for professionals with a basic understanding of business processes, aspiring to enhance their project management skills. Suitable for team leads, aspiring project managers, and business analysts looking to formalize their knowledge.

  • Basic understanding of business operations.
  • Prior involvement in project work is beneficial.
  • Open to individuals from any industry or background.
  • Commitment to learning and applying new skills.

Pre-requisites

To get the most out of this course, familiarity with fundamental project management concepts is recommended. No formal certification is required, but a keen interest in project management principles will be advantageous.

Module 01: Fundamentals of Project Management

  • - Introduction to project management principles
  • - Understanding project lifecycle and phases
  • - Roles and responsibilities of a project manager
  • - Defining project scope, objectives, and deliverables
  • - Creating a project charter and stakeholder analysis

Module 2: Project Planning and Scheduling

  • - Developing a project plan and timeline
  • - Resource allocation and task management
  • - Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) and Gantt charts
  • - Risk management and contingency planning
  • - Budgeting and cost estimation techniques

Module 03: Project Execution and Control

  • - Leading and managing project teams
  • - Communication and collaboration strategies
  • - Monitoring project progress and performance
  • - Quality management and assurance practices
  • - Managing changes and scope creep

Module 04: Project Closure and Evaluation

  • - Conducting project closure and handover
  • - Final project review and lessons learned
  • - Project documentation and archiving
  • - Post-project evaluation and continuous improvement
  • - Preparing for project management certifications (PMP, PRINCE2)

Project Management Exam and Certification

To earn the Project Management certification, candidates must complete the course requirements, including attending all training sessions, completing assignments, and passing the final exam with a minimum score of 70%.

The certification exam consists of multiple-choice questions that cover key concepts in project management, including planning, execution, monitoring, and closure. The exam duration is 2 hours, and it is administered online.

The Project Management certification is valid for three years. To maintain your certification, you must earn professional development units (PDUs) through continuous learning and professional development activities.

Preparation for the certification exam involves reviewing course materials, participating in practice tests, and studying recommended project management books. Additionally, engaging in group discussions and seeking guidance from instructors can enhance your readiness.
