Financial Modeling Course Curriculum

Eligibility

Ideal for professionals with a background in finance or business. Basic understanding of Excel is recommended.

  • Basic financial principles.
  • Excel proficiency.
  • Understanding of financial statements.
  • Analytical skills.

Pre-requisites

A basic grasp of financial concepts and Excel skills are necessary. No prior financial modeling experience is required.

Module 01: Introduction to Financial Modeling

  • - Overview of financial modeling and its applications
  • - Introduction to Excel for financial modeling
  • - Basic financial statements (Income Statement, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow Statement)
  • - Linking financial statements in a model
  • - Understanding key financial ratios and metrics

Module 02: Building a Financial Model

  • - Steps to build a financial model from scratch
  • - Revenue forecasting techniques
  • - Expense forecasting and cost analysis
  • - Working capital and cash flow forecasting
  • - Scenario and sensitivity analysis

Module 03: Advanced Financial Modeling Techniques

  • - Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) valuation
  • - Leveraged Buyout (LBO) modeling basics
  • - Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A) modeling
  • - Building complex models with macros and VBA
  • - Best practices in financial modeling

Module 04: Application and Analysis

  • - Case studies in financial modeling
  • - Industry-specific financial modeling (e.g., real estate, banking)
  • - Interpreting and presenting financial models
  • - Model auditing and error-checking techniques
  • - Preparing for financial modeling certifications

Financial Modeling Exam and Certification

The exam consists of multiple-choice questions and case studies that test your practical skills in financial modeling. It is designed to assess your ability to apply theoretical knowledge in real-world scenarios.

The certification is valid for three years from the date of issuance. After this period, you will need to renew your certification by taking a refresher course or passing a re-certification exam.

The exam covers a range of topics including financial statements analysis, forecasting, valuation techniques, and scenario modeling. It also includes practical exercises on building and interpreting financial models.

To prepare for the exam, we recommend reviewing the course materials thoroughly, practicing with sample questions, and participating in study groups or workshops. Additionally, hands-on experience with financial models will be beneficial.
